2
Nature of Counseling with Young People

Counseling provides a safe, supportive space for your child to express themselves and develop coping skills, sometimes through talking, play, or creative activities appropriate to their age.

Sessions can be conducted online or in person, as mutually agreed. Each session is typically 50 minutes, held once a week. The total number of sessions will be reviewed together based on your child's progress and needs.

3
Confidentiality & Parental Involvement

For counseling to be effective, the young person needs to feel their conversations are private. At the same time, as a parent or guardian, you have a right to be appropriately informed about your child's wellbeing and progress.

To balance these, the general approach is:

  • ◈The specific content of sessions is kept private to support the child's trust and openness
  • ◈You will receive general updates on themes, progress, and how you can support your child at home
  • ◈Confidentiality will be broken to inform you and/or relevant authorities if there is a risk of serious harm to your child or others, any disclosure of abuse, or if required by law
  • ◈Case material may be discussed confidentially in clinical supervision
Safety first: If anything raises concern for your child's safety or wellbeing, you (or the appropriate authorities) will be informed. Counseling will never be used to keep safety concerns from a parent or guardian.
4
Online & In-Person Guidelines
  • ◈For online sessions, please help your child join from a private, quiet space with a stable connection
  • ◈A parent/guardian should be contactable nearby during the session, especially for younger children
  • ◈Sessions will not be recorded by either party without prior written consent
  • ◈For in-person sessions, venue and arrangements will be agreed with you in advance
5
Your Rights & Emergencies
  • ◈You may withdraw your child from counseling at any time
  • ◈You may ask questions about the process at any point
  • ◈You have the right to a planned ending wherever possible
  • ◈This is not an emergency service; in a crisis please contact your local emergency services or a mental health helpline available in your region

A Note Just for You

Hi! My name is Keerti. I'm someone you can talk to about your feelings, worries, or anything on your mind. We might talk, draw, play, or do activities together — whatever feels okay for you.

Here are some things I want you to know:

  • ★You don't have to talk about anything you're not ready to talk about.
  • ★What we talk about is mostly private between us.
  • ★But if I'm ever worried that you or someone else might get hurt, I will need to tell a grown-up who can help keep you safe — and I'll try to tell you first.
  • ★It's okay to tell me if something feels uncomfortable, or if you don't want to come. Your feelings matter here.
  • ★There are no wrong answers, and you won't be in trouble for anything you share.

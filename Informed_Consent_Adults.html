Nature of Counseling

Counseling is a collaborative process. Together we will explore your thoughts, feelings, and experiences in a safe, non-judgmental space, working toward insight, coping, and the changes you want in your life.

Counseling is not a quick fix and may at times bring up difficult emotions — a natural part of the process. You remain in control of what you share and at what pace.

  • ◈Sessions can be conducted online (video call) or in person, as mutually agreed
  • ◈Each session is typically 50 minutes, held once a week at a mutually agreed time
  • ◈The total number of sessions will be discussed based on your needs and reviewed as we go
  • ◈Sessions are offered on a pro bono basis — there are no fees
3
Clinical Supervision

As part of ethical practice, I receive regular clinical supervision from Anupama Ganu (Play Therapist). This means aspects of our work may be discussed in supervision to ensure you receive safe and effective support.

Your identity and personal details are kept confidential within supervision; my supervisor is also bound by professional ethics and confidentiality. Supervision is a standard and protective part of counseling practice.

What this means for you: The content of our sessions may be discussed confidentially with my supervisor. This is for your benefit and is a routine, ethical part of supervised practice — not a breach of your privacy.
4
Confidentiality & Its Limits

Everything you share is confidential. I will not disclose your information without your written consent — except in the following ethically and legally required situations:

  • ◈Risk of serious harm to yourself where you are unable to stay safe
  • ◈Risk of serious harm to another person
  • ◈Disclosure or risk of harm to a child or vulnerable adult
  • ◈If disclosure is required by law or a court order
  • ◈Case material shared in clinical supervision (Section 3)
Where your safety or another's is at risk, I will aim to discuss any necessary action with you first wherever it is safe and possible to do so.
5
Online & In-Person Session Guidelines

For online sessions:

  • ◈Please be in a private, quiet space where you will not be overheard
  • ◈Use headphones where possible for privacy
  • ◈Sessions will not be recorded by either party without prior written consent
  • ◈Ensure a stable internet connection; if disconnected, I will attempt to reconnect or contact you shortly
  • ◈Please do not attend while driving or in a public place

For in-person sessions: The venue, timing, and any practical arrangements will be communicated and agreed with you in advance.

6
Cancellations & Missed Sessions
  • ◈Please give at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule, so the time can be offered to someone else
  • ◈If you miss two or more consecutive sessions without notice, we will need to have a conversation about whether to continue
  • ◈I will equally give you at least 24 hours' notice if I need to reschedule
  • ◈Cancellations can be communicated via WhatsApp message
7
Contact Between Sessions & Emergencies

I am available for brief, practical contact (scheduling, cancellations) via WhatsApp. Messaging is not a substitute for sessions; if something urgent comes up, let me know and we can arrange an earlier session where possible.

I am not an emergency or crisis service. If you are in immediate danger or crisis between sessions, please contact your local emergency services or a mental health crisis helpline available in your region.

Your Rights as a Client
  • ◈You may withdraw from counseling at any time without giving a reason
  • ◈You may ask questions about the process at any point
  • ◈You may give feedback about your experience
  • ◈You have the right to a planned ending wherever possible
  • ◈If our work is not helping, please tell me directly so we can adjust or consider a referral
